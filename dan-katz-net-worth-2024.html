Dan Katz Net Worth 2024

Introduction

Dan Katz, also known as “Big Cat,” is a renowned American podcaster and writer, best known for his work on the sports and pop culture blog, Barstool Sports. He is the co-host of the popular podcast, Pardon My Take, which has been ranked among the top podcasts in the sports category on iTunes. Katz’s unique blend of humor, sports knowledge, and engaging personality has made him a favorite among sports fans and podcast listeners alike. As of 2024, his net worth is a topic of interest to many.

Early Life and Career Beginnings

Dan Katz was born and raised in Chicago, Illinois. He attended the University of Wisconsin-Madison, where he developed a passion for sports and broadcasting. After graduation, he began his career in the media industry, working for various local radio stations and sports blogs.

Joining Barstool Sports

In 2014, Katz joined Barstool Sports, a digital media company that produces content focused on sports and pop-culture. He quickly became one of the site’s most popular writers, known for his humorous and often irreverent takes on sports news.

Success with Pardon My Take

In 2016, Katz and his co-host PFT Commenter launched the podcast Pardon My Take under the Barstool Sports banner. The podcast quickly gained popularity, becoming one of the top sports podcasts on iTunes within a few months of its launch.

Podcast Revenue

One of the primary sources of Katz’s wealth is the revenue generated from Pardon My Take. The podcast’s success has led to lucrative advertising deals, with sponsors paying premium rates to reach the show’s dedicated audience.

Other Ventures

Beyond his work with Barstool Sports and Pardon My Take, Katz has also ventured into other areas. He has made appearances on various television shows and has even tried his hand at acting.

Television Appearances

Katz has made guest appearances on several sports talk shows, including ESPN’s Around the Horn and Fox Sports’ The Herd with Colin Cowherd. These appearances have not only increased his visibility but also added to his income.

Acting Career

In 2018, Katz made his acting debut in the short film “Sword of Trust.” While his acting career is still in its early stages, it represents another potential source of income for Katz.

Personal Life

Philanthropy

Future Earnings Potential

Given the continued success of Pardon My Take and Katz’s growing popularity, it is likely that his net worth will continue to increase in the coming years. His ventures into television and acting also represent potential avenues for future earnings.

Investments
