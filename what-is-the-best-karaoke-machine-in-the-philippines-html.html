Who created the karaoke?

The world’s first karaoke machine, the Juke-8, was invented by Japanese inventor and musician Daisuke Inoue in 1971. But it is Filipino inventor Roberto del Rosario who possesses the machine’s patent. He invented the Karaoke Sing-Along System in 1975.

What are karaoke machines?

A simple karaoke machine comprises of a music player, microphone inputs, a mechanism of adjusting the pitch of the played song, and an audio output. Most typical devices are CD+G, Laser Disc, VCD or DVD players with microphone inputs and an audio mixer built in.

How do I achieve a high score on karaoke?

Most inexpensive karaoke systems are able to assess your singing by your timing and the strength of your voice. If you sing at the proper time and in a louder voice, you gain a greater score. This implies that if you sing louder, without even reaching the appropriate note, you’ll still earn a high score.
